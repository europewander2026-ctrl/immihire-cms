Common Questions

A Work Permit is a temporary authorization tied to a specific employer or time frame (e.g., Schengen Work Permit). Permanent Residency (PR), like Canada Express Entry or Australia Subclass 189, grants you the right to live, work, and study anywhere in the country indefinitely, with access to healthcare and social benefits.
The most effective ways to boost your CRS score are: 1) Retaking IELTS to achieve CLB 9+ (8777), 2) Learning French (TEF exam), 3) Obtaining a Provincial Nomination (PNP) which adds 600 points, or 4) Gaining more skilled work experience. ImmiHire provides dedicated strategies for each.
While high-level German is not strictly required for the visa application itself (English A2 or German A1 is often sufficient for points), knowing German significantly improves your job prospects. For the Chancenkarte, you need 6 points total, and language skills are a key contributor to this score.
Australia's 2025 migration strategy prioritizes Healthcare (Nurses, GPs), Construction/Trades (Electricians, Carpenters), Teaching, and IT/Cybersecurity. If your occupation is on the Medium and Long-term Strategic Skills List (MLTSSL), you have a higher chance of invitation.
For many nationalities, the US B1/B2 visa is issued for **10 years** as a multiple-entry visa. However, the maximum duration of stay per visit is determined by the CBP officer at the port of entry, typically up to 6 months. ImmiHire assists with DS-160 filing and interview preparation.
Currently, Poland, Lithuania, and Czech Republic have more accessible work permit routes for non-EU citizens due to labor shortages in logistics, manufacturing, and construction. Processing times can be as fast as 3-4 months.
